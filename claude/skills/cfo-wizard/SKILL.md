## Main Protocol: Quick Tax Operations

**Home Office Rent (Monthly):**
1. Calculate: $5,000/month (2 rooms at 899 Congress St)
2. Execute: Mercury payment request (personal → business)
3. Document: Screenshot PAID status + save link
4. Track: Update transfer log

**Augusta Rule (Annual, ≤14 days):**
1. Plan: Business retreat with clear agenda
2. Document: Attendees, business purpose, schedule
3. Rate: $3,000-$5,000 (market-comparable)
4. Execute: Payment request with IRC 280A(g) citation
5. Package: Agenda + attendees + memo + payment proof
6. Year-end: Provide to Brad, do NOT report as personal income

**IRS Notice Response:**
1. Don't panic (most are routine)
2. Gather evidence (bank statements, receipts, correct 1099s)
3. Calculate correct amount
4. Draft response with evidence
5. Brad review before mailing
6. Mail certified (return receipt)
7. Track deadline (buffer 2 weeks)

## Critical Patterns

**✅ DO THIS:**
- Conservative calculations (don't inflate)
- Arms-length transactions (market rates)
- Clear documentation (photos, contracts, memos)
- Payment requests (not just ACH transfers) - creates audit trail
- Track everything (Mercury log, Ramp receipts, Gusto summaries)

**❌ NEVER DO THIS:**
- Round numbers without justification
- 100% business use claims (cars, phones)
- Missing documentation (no receipts/proof)
- Ignore IRS notices (respond with evidence)
- Personal expenses on business card (reimburse immediately)

**Key Insights:**
- Home office + Augusta Rule **STACK** (use both in same year!)
- Payment requests > ACH transfers (formal invoice = better audit defense)
- Solo 401k > IRA ($70K vs $7K contribution limit)
- Cash vs accrual timing (common 1099-K mismatch cause)
- Conservative R&D credits (sovereignty > maximum credit)
