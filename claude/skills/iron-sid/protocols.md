## WARMUP PROTOCOL - STANDING ONLY

**CRITICAL**: No mat work. Standing = no friction to skip.

### Phase 1: Glute Activation (3 min) - FIRST, ALWAYS

**Problem**: Anterior pelvic tilt (weak glutes â†’ lower back compensates)
**Detection**: Lower back feeling before workout = glutes not firing

1. **Standing Glute Squeezes**: 20 reps
   - Squeeze HARD 2 seconds each
   - Wake up sleeping stabilizers
   - Fixes pelvic tilt pattern

2. **Single-Leg RDL Bodyweight**: 10 each leg
   - Fires gluteus medius (the lower back culprit + knee stability)
   - Hold wall/rack for balance if needed
   - Prevents knee collapse pattern

3. **Wall Planks**: 30 seconds
   - Hands on wall, walk feet back, lean in
   - Core bracing practice
   - Core tight = back safe

### Phase 2: Movement Prep (2 min) - AFTER glutes activated
1. **Arm Circles**: 10 forward/10 back
2. **Shoulder Rolls**: 10 each direction
3. **Dead Hang**: 10-20 seconds (scapula engagement, grip activation)
4. **Wall Push-ups**: 10 reps [Push days]
5. **Light DB Press**: 10-15 lbs x 10 reps [Push days]

### Phase 3: Recovery Tool (Post-Workout) - MANDATORY
**Dead Hangs**: 20-30 seconds
- "Spine elongating in real time. Just to feel alpha"
- Decompress spine after loaded movements
- Pre-workout: 10-20 sec (engagement)
- Post-workout: 20-30 sec (decompression)

**Success Metric**: Glutes firing, shoulders loose, core braced, lower back feeling gone.

## SAFETY PROTOCOLS

### Injury Prevention
- **Wrist issues**: Always use push-up handles
- **Lower back**: Glute activation + wide stance on ALL standing exercises
- **Core engagement**: Active bracing on all lifts
- **Form over weight**: 25lbs good form > 35lbs bad form

### Wide Stance Principle
Universal fix for lower back pressure:
- Applies to: Biceps curls, face pulls, squats, RDLs, all standing movements
- Wider stance = core engaged automatically
- "Biceps wide stance really made these last 2 exercises not annoy me"

### Recovery Rules
- 2-3 days optimal between sessions
- Extended breaks OK if protein maintained
- Weekend indulgences: Don't panic, movement flushes systems
- Soreness = progress (especially hamstrings)
